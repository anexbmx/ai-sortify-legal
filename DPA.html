Data Processing Agreement

AI Sortify

Last updated: June 17, 2026

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") applies when AI Sortify processes personal data on behalf of a customer or workspace in connection with the AI Sortify service. It forms part of the Terms of Service unless a separate written data processing agreement is signed.

1. Parties and roles

Customer / Controller: the person, company, organization, or workspace administrator that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data uploaded to or connected with AI Sortify.

AI Sortify / Processor: AI Sortify, operated by Anas Boumediene, processes customer personal data only to provide and support the service, according to customer instructions, this DPA, the Terms, and applicable law.

2. Subject matter and duration

The subject matter is the processing of personal data for AI-assisted document classification, extraction, renaming, splitting, folder generation, Google Drive workflows, workspace/team features, subscription enforcement, support, security, and related service operation.

Processing continues for the duration of the customer's use of AI Sortify and any additional period required for deletion, backup rotation, legal obligations, dispute resolution, or security purposes.

3. Nature and purpose of processing

Processing activity Purpose
Collection, upload, retrieval, storage, and transmission Receive documents, metadata, workspace records, and user instructions for requested workflows.
Extraction, classification, transformation, and AI analysis Extract text or fields, classify documents, suggest filenames, route files, and generate workflow outputs.
Google Drive actions Create folders, read selected metadata, move/rename selected files, and maintain Drive connection state.
Access control and workspace administration Authenticate users, manage teams, enforce permissions, and protect workspaces.
Logging, diagnostics, and support Operate, troubleshoot, secure, and improve reliability of the service.
Deletion, anonymization, backup, and export Handle user/customer deletion, disconnection, export, portability, and backup lifecycle requests.

4. Categories of personal data

  • Account identifiers: name, email, user ID, avatar, language, authentication metadata.
  • Workspace data: organization name, team members, roles, invitations, settings.
  • Document content: PDFs, images, scans, spreadsheets, extracted text, filenames, metadata, classification outputs.
  • Google Drive data: account email, OAuth tokens, folder IDs, file IDs, filenames, MIME types, folder paths, selected file/folder metadata.
  • AI workflow data: prompts/instructions, extracted fields, classification labels, suggested filenames, confidence/review status.
  • Subscription and usage data: plan, entitlement, usage counts, quota data, purchase status.
  • Technical and security data: IP addresses in logs, request metadata, device/app metadata, error logs, audit/security records.
  • Support data: messages, screenshots or documents provided for support, deletion requests, legal requests.

5. Categories of data subjects

  • Customer users and workspace administrators.
  • Customer employees, contractors, clients, suppliers, or business contacts appearing in uploaded documents.
  • Team invitees and support contacts.
  • Individuals whose personal data appears in documents, filenames, folders, metadata, or user instructions.

6. Customer instructions

Customer instructs AI Sortify to process personal data as needed to provide the service and as configured or initiated by users in the app. AI Sortify will not process customer personal data for unrelated purposes unless required by law. If AI Sortify believes an instruction violates applicable data protection law, it may suspend the instruction and inform the customer where legally permitted.

7. Confidentiality

AI Sortify restricts access to personal data to persons and providers that need access to operate, secure, support, or maintain the service. Persons with access are required to maintain confidentiality by contract, professional duty, or equivalent obligation.

10. International transfers

Subprocessors may process personal data outside the customer's country. AI Sortify relies on appropriate safeguards where required, such as standard contractual clauses, adequacy decisions, data processing agreements, or other lawful transfer mechanisms.

11. Assistance with data subject requests

AI Sortify will reasonably assist customers with data subject access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, and objection requests to the extent technically possible and legally required. Customers remain responsible for determining whether and how to respond to requests relating to their own documents and workspace data.

12. Assistance with compliance

Taking into account the nature of processing and information available to AI Sortify, AI Sortify will reasonably assist customers with security, breach notification, data protection impact assessment, and regulator consultation obligations where required by applicable data protection law.

13. Personal data breach

If AI Sortify becomes aware of a personal data breach affecting customer personal data, it will notify affected customers without undue delay where legally required and provide information reasonably available to support customer notification obligations.

14. Deletion and return

Upon account deletion, workspace deletion, Drive disconnection, or written request, AI Sortify will delete or return customer personal data where technically possible, unless retention is required for legal, security, backup, dispute, or legitimate operational reasons. Backup deletion may take additional time according to backup rotation schedules.

15. Audits and information

AI Sortify will make reasonably necessary information available to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, subject to confidentiality, security, and protection of other customers. Any audit must be reasonable in scope, frequency, timing, and method, and must not compromise service security or third-party confidentiality obligations.

16. Customer responsibilities

  • Customer must have a lawful basis for all personal data uploaded, connected, or processed through AI Sortify.
  • Customer must provide required notices to data subjects and obtain required consents or authorizations.
  • Customer must configure Google Drive, team roles, permissions, and workflows responsibly.
  • Customer must review AI outputs and is responsible for final decisions based on those outputs.
  • Customer must not upload unlawful content or content that AI Sortify is not designed to process.

17. Conflict

If this DPA conflicts with the Terms of Service, this DPA controls only for processing of personal data on behalf of a customer. A signed written agreement controls over this posted DPA where it expressly says so.
